Equity Through Amortization

Equity through Amortization is simply the home equity that is determined by taking the difference in the purchase price of the house and the loan balance of the mortgage. As you make your monthly mortgage payment, part of each payment is the principal payment that lowers the principal balance or loan balance converting principal into equity. In a sense, this is a forced savings plan. On the left is what your Amortization Chart will look like based on the $412,284 Purchase Price with 20% down payment ($329,827 Loan Amount).

Equity Through Appreciation

Housing Appreciation is the increase in value of your house. Determining this can be tricky so I suggest speaking to your Realtor every year or two and get a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA). This can help us determine the rate of appreciation of your house more accurately as well as how much wealth is created by simply increase in house value. According to Federal Housing Finance Agency and its House Price Index for New Jersey, average housing appreciation is 3.425% per year over the past 5 years. The chart on the right shows how much wealth can be created if this rate of appreciation continues in the future.

Tax Benefits

There may or may not be Tax Benefits for paying interest and property taxes on a house. It all depends on your tax bracket and how you are filing your taxes. To learn about any tax benefits for owning a house, please consult with your Tax Accountant.
